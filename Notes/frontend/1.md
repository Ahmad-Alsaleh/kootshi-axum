- The app is a Dual-sided marketplace design.
- Players are the demand Pitch Owners are the the supply.
- The player side of the app is used to explore, book, and pay for pitches.
- The pitch owners side of the app is used as a management dashboard.

---

## 1. Core User Journeys

### For Players

* **Discovery:** A map-based or list-based view showing nearby pitches with filters for sport type (football, padel, etc.), surface (grass, hardcourt), and price.
* **Instant Booking:** View real-time availability in 60 or 90-minute "slots."
* **Matchmaking (later):** A "Join a Game" feature for individuals looking for a team.
* **Payment:** Split-payment functionality (so one person doesn't have to front the whole cost).

### For Pitch Owners

* **Inventory Management:** Toggle slots on/off (e.g., closing a pitch for maintenance).
* **Dynamic Pricing (later):** Higher rates for "Peak" times (weeknights) and discounts for "Off-peak" (weekday mornings).
* **Analytics:** View monthly revenue, most popular hours, and no-show rates.

## 3. The Tech Stack (Recommended)

To ensure the app is fast and scalable, I recommend a modern cross-platform approach:

* **Frontend:** **Flutter** or **React Native** (One codebase for both iOS and Android).
* **Backend:** **Node.js** or **Python (FastAPI)** for high-concurrency booking requests.
* **Database:** **PostgreSQL** (for structured booking data) with **PostGIS** for location-based searching.
* **Maps:** **Google Maps Platform** or **Mapbox** for the pitch discovery interface.
* **Payments:** **Stripe Connect** (specifically designed for marketplaces to split payments between the platform and owners).

---

## 4. Proposed Revenue Model (important)

How you can monetize the platform:

1. **Commission Fee:** A 5–10% service fee on every booking.
2. **Premium Listings:** Pitch owners pay to be featured at the top of search results.
3. **SaaS Subscription:** Charge owners a monthly fee for advanced management tools (e.g., automated lighting integration).
4. **In-App Ads:** Partner with sports brands (Nike, Adidas) for targeted ads.

---

## 5. Design Considerations (UI/UX)

* **The "3-Tap" Rule:** A user should be able to book a pitch in 3 taps from the home screen.
* **Visual Trust (important):** High-quality image galleries for pitches are the #1 driver of conversions.
* **Availability Grid:** Use a clear, color-coded time grid (Green = Available, Red = Booked).

> **Pro-Tip:** Include a "Recurring Booking" feature for local leagues or groups of friends who play every Tuesday at 7 PM. This creates "sticky" revenue for the owners and convenience for the players.
